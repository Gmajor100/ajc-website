His Story

A Life Well Lived

Anyanwu Jonathan Chukwuma — known to all who loved him as AJC — was born in Plateau State, Nigeria, into a family rooted in faith, hard work, and deep Igbo tradition. From his earliest days, he carried himself with the kind of quiet dignity that commands respect without demanding it.

"He walked into every room and the atmosphere changed — not because he was loud, but because he was present."

A trained and accomplished architect, AJC spent his career shaping spaces and building structures that outlasted him — a fitting legacy for a man who spent his life building people too. After retirement, he devoted himself entirely to his faith, his family, and his community, becoming a beloved father figure to all who knew him.

As one of Jehovah's Witnesses, his faith was not merely a part of his life — it was the foundation of it. He studied the Scriptures diligently, attended every meeting with faithfulness, and lived out the principles he believed in with remarkable consistency. He was jovial, warm, and endlessly encouraging — a man whose laughter could fill a room and whose counsel could settle a storm.

He is survived by his 5 beloved children, his grandchildren, and a community of people whose lives he touched and transformed. AJC did not just live — he gave life to everyone around him.
