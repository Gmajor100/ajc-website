His Story

A Life Well Lived

Anyanwu Jonathan Chukwuma — known to all who loved him as AJC — was born on the 1st of February, 1938, in Amaeke Ineku, Umuahia, Abia State, into a family rooted in faith, hard work, and deep Igbo tradition. He spent the greater part of his life in Jos, Plateau State, where he built a legacy that extended far beyond the walls of any structure he ever designed.

"Education is a prerequisite that enables one to hold the target of life." — AJC, to his grandchildren

AJC received his early education at Mission Hill School, and went on to qualify as an architect through the British Education System — a remarkable achievement that defined much of his professional life. He practised his craft with quiet excellence for decades, retiring around the age of 70, at which point he made a deliberate and joyful decision: to devote the remainder of his days entirely to the service of Jehovah.

As one of Jehovah's Witnesses, his faith was never merely a part of his life — it was the very foundation of it. He was known above all as a peacemaker, a man who could walk into any conflict and walk out having left harmony behind. His hobbies were singing and reading, and his favourite scripture — Proverbs 10:22 — reflected the way he lived: trusting fully in Jehovah's blessing, never grasping, never anxious.

He is survived by his 5 beloved children, his many grandchildren, and a wide community of people whose lives he touched and transformed. AJC did not merely live 87 years — he filled every one of them with purpose, faith, and love.
